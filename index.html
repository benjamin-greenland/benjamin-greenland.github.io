About Me

Hi, I’m Ben Greenland, a PhD researcher specialising in soft robotics. My research focuses on a fundamental feature in robotics: balancing the high-dimensional complexity of human-like dexterity with the elegant simplicity required for robust, real-world soft robotic systems.

Traditional rigid robotic arms often rely on a massive number of motors and complex mechatronic parts,making them heavy, expensive, and fragile. My work flips this script by leveraging morphological computation—shifting mechanical and computational complexity away from the controller and electronics directly into the physical, compliant structure of the manipulator itself.

My research follows a clear progression across three core pillars:

  • Hardware & Compliance: Investigating underactuated design methodologies that reduce mechatronic complexity while ensuring robots can safely adapt to varied object geometries and transition between fragile handling and high-force tasks.
  • Performance Evaluation: Developing evaluation metrics and experimental protocols to assess the in-hand manipulation performance of soft robotic systems.
  • Design Optimisation: Utilising computational frameworks to evaluate how physical finger geometry and sensor configurations can be concurrently structured to maximise system performance.

Ultimately, my goal is to help build formal computational frameworks that prove robots can be physically compliant, mechatronically streamlined, and highly intelligent all at the same time.
